Facebook

Facebook is the third most popular website in the world (http://www.alexa.com/topsites). This means that it both rivals the range of other social networks and any other website available on the internet. This is especially important to note, as Facebook continues to expand its services available to both its users and the businesses and advertisers that use the available tools.

To start on Facebook, you will need a personal profile. This profile will allow you to create pages, manage adverts, and build a friends list.

Setting up a Facebook Page

Facebook has built some great resources walking through the process of setting up a page. Some of the most important components are a Facebook Cover photo, Profile Photo and business information.

Start setting up your Facebok page here

Facebook Insights

Once you have some page likes, and posted some content, you'll get access to Facebook Insights. If you have heard of Google Analytics, it's pretty much that for Facebook pages. Insights will give you an overview of your audience demographics - with stats gender, age, and location. You will be able to see how your posts are performing and how your followers interact with this content.

Facebook Content Types

The structured nature of Facebook allows for content to remain similar across all publishers - which is an important step to limit bias and provide a fair go to all users. There is a range of content types available to use, which will format your information in a user-friendly way, and encourage them to interact with it.

  • Text Post: Ideal for sharing information, keep it short, sharp and engaging.
  • Photo/Video: One of the most engaging post types. Images will feature the full width of the News Feed, and videos will start playing automatically to engage users as they scroll.
  • Ads: Reach people who aren't already connected to your page. Target audience types, age ranges and interests.
  • Get Phone Calls: Encourage your audience to get in touch with your business. This will allow people to click a link that will automatically start a call to the phone number attached to the page.
  • Get Messages: Similar to the Get Phone Calls post, Facebook will allow users to directly message the Page.
  • Events: Facebook Events will build a community surrounding a particular event. Communicate directly with people who have responded to the event.
  • Offers: With a built in offer reddeming process, users can claim coupons to your products or services. It's a good way to connect with people who may like your page, but don't interract with your content.
  • Notes: Notes are basically long text posts. Use these to hold longer content if you don't have anywhere else for them to be stored. Common uses are Terms and Conditions for competitions that are run on Facebook.
