WordPress

WordPress is an open source Content Management System (CMS). As an open source platform, a wide variety of developers contribute to the maintenance and building of the overall framework and allows the collective expertise to develop a refined system.

Wordpress itself began as a blogging platform, allowing users to publish regular updates and comment on a network of blogs by other users. As users looked to expand on this service, Wordpress.org was developed. This platform expanded with a selection of user-contributed plugins to expand its functionality with integrations into different web services, analytics, forms, CRM (Customer Relationship Management) services.

Another key feature of WordPress is its theme marketplace. Similar to the plugin library, WordPress themes are developed by a range of authors. Themes are categorised by industry and there are hundreds of themes available - all with a range of inbuilt functionality such as accommodation reservations, event management and forums.

Starting out, WordPress sites are relatively simple to set up.

  1. Find a WordPress host (see the Websites page) and get an account set up.
  2. Find and install a theme. Use the inbuilt Theme Customiser (or your theme's specific layout options) to style your site.
  3. Add your content. By default, WordPress uses two different formats - pages, which are static, and posts, which are regularly updated. Different themes may expand on these post types. Wordpress also includes an inbuilt media manager for photos, videos, audio and other files.
  4. Expand your site's functionality through plugins. Some good ones include Yoast SEO, Contact Form 7 and Google Analyticator.
